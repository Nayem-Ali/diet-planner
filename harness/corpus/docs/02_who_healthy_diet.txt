[[section: Free sugars]]
Keep intake of free sugars to less than 10 percent of total energy intake (a
strong recommendation). A further reduction to below 5 percent of total energy
intake provides additional health benefits, including reduced dental caries
(conditional recommendation). Free sugars include monosaccharides and
disaccharides added to foods, plus sugars naturally present in honey, syrups, and
fruit juices.

[[section: Sodium]]
Adults should consume less than 2,000 mg of sodium per day, equivalent to less
than 5 grams of salt (about one teaspoon) per day. Reducing sodium intake lowers
blood pressure and the risk of cardiovascular disease, stroke, and coronary heart
disease. (Source: WHO Sodium Reduction fact sheet,
https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/sodium-reduction)

[[section: Potassium]]
Adults should consume at least 3,510 mg of potassium per day (90 mmol/day) from
food, which helps lower blood pressure and cardiovascular risk. Fruits and
vegetables are key sources.

[[section: Fruit and vegetables]]
Eat at least 400 grams of fruit and vegetables per day (five portions), excluding
starchy roots such as potatoes, to reduce the risk of noncommunicable disease and
ensure adequate fibre intake.

[[section: Total and saturated fat]]
Keep total fat to no more than 30 percent of total energy intake, with saturated
fats below 10 percent and trans fats below 1 percent of total energy intake;
shift consumption toward unsaturated fats.
