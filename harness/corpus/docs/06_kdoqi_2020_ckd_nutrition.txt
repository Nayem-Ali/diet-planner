[[section: Protein, non-dialysis CKD]]
For adults with CKD stages 3-5 who are not on dialysis and are metabolically
stable, restrict dietary protein to 0.55 to 0.60 g/kg ideal body weight per day
(with appropriate education and monitoring) to reduce risk of progression and
uremic symptoms.

[[section: Protein, CKD with diabetes]]
For adults with CKD stages 3-5 who have diabetes and are not on dialysis,
prescribe a dietary protein intake of 0.6 to 0.8 g/kg ideal body weight per day to
maintain stable nutritional status and optimize glycemic control.

[[section: Protein, maintenance dialysis]]
For adults on maintenance hemodialysis or peritoneal dialysis who are
metabolically stable, prescribe a dietary protein intake of 1.0 to 1.2 g/kg ideal
body weight per day to maintain a stable nutritional status, compensating for
dialytic amino-acid and protein losses.

[[section: Sodium]]
Limit sodium intake to less than 2.3 g (2,300 mg) per day to reduce blood pressure
and improve volume control in adults with CKD.

[[section: Potassium and phosphorus]]
Adjust dietary potassium intake to maintain serum potassium within the normal
range, individualized to the patient. Adjust dietary phosphorus intake to maintain
serum phosphate within the normal range; this is individualized rather than a
single fixed milligram target (a common practical range is about 800 to 1,000 mg
per day when restriction is indicated).
