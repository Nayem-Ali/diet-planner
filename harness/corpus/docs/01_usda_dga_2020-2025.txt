[[section: Metabolizable energy (Atwater) factors]]
Metabolizable energy is estimated with the general Atwater factors: protein
provides about 4 kcal per gram, carbohydrate about 4 kcal per gram, total fat
about 9 kcal per gram, and alcohol (ethanol) about 7 kcal per gram. These
factors are the standard basis for energy values in U.S. food composition data
(USDA FoodData Central) and for calorie totals in diet-planning tools.

[[section: Added sugars]]
Limit added sugars to less than 10 percent of total daily calories, starting at
age 2. Added sugars are sugars added during processing or preparation; naturally
occurring sugars in intact fruits, vegetables, and plain dairy are not counted.

[[section: Saturated fat]]
Limit saturated fat to less than 10 percent of total daily calories, starting at
age 2, replacing it with unsaturated fats where possible.

[[section: Sodium]]
Limit sodium to less than 2,300 mg per day for most adults and children age 14
and older (about one teaspoon of table salt). This is the U.S. dietary-guidelines
upper limit; the World Health Organization sets a lower target (see WHO source).

[[section: Alcohol]]
For adults who choose to drink, limit intake to 2 drinks or less per day for men
and 1 drink or less per day for women. Drinking less is better for health than
drinking more; some adults should not drink at all.

[[section: Dietary pattern]]
Follow a healthy dietary pattern across life stages: emphasize vegetables,
fruits, whole grains, fat-free or low-fat dairy, a variety of protein foods, and
oils, while limiting added sugars, saturated fat, sodium, and alcoholic beverages.
