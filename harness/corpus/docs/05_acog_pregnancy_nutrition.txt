[[section: Energy in pregnancy]]
Energy needs do not increase in the first trimester (about 0 additional kcal/day).
Estimated additional energy is about 340 kcal per day in the second trimester and
about 452 kcal per day in the third trimester (IOM estimates).

[[section: Folic acid]]
During pregnancy, aim for 600 micrograms of folic acid per day. Because this is
hard to reach from food alone, take a prenatal vitamin with at least 400
micrograms of folic acid daily, starting at least 1 month before pregnancy and
through the first 12 weeks, to reduce the risk of neural tube defects.

[[section: Iron]]
Pregnant women need about 27 mg of iron per day to support expanded maternal
blood volume and fetal development, typically provided through prenatal vitamins.

[[section: Caffeine]]
Limit caffeine to less than 200 mg per day during pregnancy (roughly one 12-oz cup
of coffee).

[[section: Foods to avoid]]
Avoid unpasteurized milk and soft cheeses, raw or undercooked seafood/meat/eggs,
and unheated deli meats and hot dogs, because of Listeria and other foodborne
risks. Limit high-mercury fish (shark, swordfish, king mackerel, tilefish) and
choose lower-mercury fish in moderation.
