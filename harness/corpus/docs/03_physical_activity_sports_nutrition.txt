[[section: Aerobic activity]]
Adults should do at least 150 to 300 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic
physical activity per week, or 75 to 150 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic
activity, or an equivalent combination. Additional benefits accrue beyond 300
minutes of moderate activity.

[[section: Muscle strengthening]]
Adults should do muscle-strengthening activities involving all major muscle
groups on 2 or more days per week, as these provide additional health benefits.

[[section: Carbohydrate for training]]
Daily carbohydrate needs scale with training volume (ACSM/ISSN): light training
about 3 to 5 g/kg body weight/day; moderate (~1 h/day) 5 to 7 g/kg/day; moderate
to high endurance (1 to 3 h/day) 6 to 10 g/kg/day; very high volume (4 to 5 h/day)
8 to 12 g/kg/day. After hard exercise, carbohydrate plus protein supports glycogen
repletion.

[[section: Protein for training]]
Protein recommendations (ISSN/ACSM): the general adult RDA is 0.8 g/kg/day;
endurance athletes about 1.2 to 1.4 g/kg/day; for building and maintaining muscle,
1.4 to 2.0 g/kg/day (commonly cited up to ~2.2 g/kg/day). To maximize muscle
protein synthesis, distribute protein as roughly 20 to 40 g doses every 3 to 4
hours, including after exercise. During energy restriction, higher protein intakes
help preserve lean mass.

[[section: Exercise hydration]]
Prehydrate by drinking about 5 to 7 mL/kg body weight at least 4 hours before
exercise. During exercise, drink to limit body-mass loss to under 2 percent; for
sessions over 60 minutes, fluids containing sodium (about 0.5 to 0.7 g/L) and
carbohydrate help sustain performance and reduce hyponatremia risk. After
exercise, replace each kilogram of body mass lost with about 1.25 to 1.5 L of
fluid.
