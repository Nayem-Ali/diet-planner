[[section: Total water]]
The Adequate Intake for total water (from beverages and food combined) is about
2.7 liters per day for adult women and about 3.7 liters per day for adult men.
Roughly 80 percent of total water typically comes from beverages and 20 percent
from food. Needs rise with heat, humidity, and physical activity.

[[section: Dietary fiber]]
The Adequate Intake for total fiber is 14 grams per 1,000 kcal consumed. For
adults aged 19 to 50, this corresponds to about 25 grams per day for women and 38
grams per day for men. Fiber supports laxation, satiety, and blood-glucose and
serum-cholesterol regulation.

[[section: Protein RDA]]
The Recommended Dietary Allowance for protein in healthy adults is 0.8 g/kg body
weight per day. Acceptable Macronutrient Distribution Ranges are 45 to 65 percent
of energy from carbohydrate, 20 to 35 percent from fat, and 10 to 35 percent from
protein.

[[section: Safe weight loss]]
A gradual rate of weight loss of about 0.5 to 1 kg (1 to 2 lb) per week is
generally considered safe and sustainable, achieved through an energy deficit of
roughly 500 to 1,000 kcal per day below total daily energy expenditure. Very low
energy availability (below about 30 kcal per kg of fat-free mass per day) can
disrupt endocrine and bone health and should be avoided.
